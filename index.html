About Matcha

The Story and Benefit of Matcha

Matcha originated in China during the Tang Dynasty (7th to 10th centuries)

where tea leaves were steamed, dried, and ground into a powder to be mixed with hot water. However, it was in Japan that matcha developed into the form we know today,The Japanese refined the cultivation and preparation of matcha, incorporating it into the traditional tea ceremony (chanoyu)

Benefit of Matcha

Matcha offers numerous health benefits, including boosting metabolism, enhancing brain function, and providing antioxidants that protect against diseases. It may also support heart health and aid in weight management.

Ceremonial grade

Premium Grade Matcha

Premium Grade Matcha, also known as usucha or "thin tea," is often used for everyday consumption, including traditional brewing, lattes, and smoothies

Culinary grade

culinary grade is more affordable and suitable for cooking and baking

Reasons to Consume Matcha

Consuming matcha is beneficial due to its high antioxidant content, which helps combat free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. It can boost metabolism and promote fat burning, making it a great choice for weight management

Additionally, matcha contains L-theanine, an amino acid that promotes relaxation and enhances focus without the jitters associated with caffeine. It also supports the immune system and has detoxifying properties that help cleanse the body of toxins.
